What is malaria and why are we looking for malarial parasites?

Malaria is a serious tropical disease usually spread by mosquitoes. More particularly, a parasite called Plasmodium, which is carried by a particular type of mosquito. When the mosquito bites people, the malaria parasites will be transmitted to the bloodstream. The typical symptoms of malaria include high fever, headache, nausea, vomiting, tiredness, coma, bloody stools, etc.

Malaria can be life-threatening when causing severe anaemia and brain damage. According to The World Health Organisation (WHO), there are about 300 million cased of malaria, and 584,000 people died because of it in 2013.

How is vision and imaging science helping to detect malarial parasites?

Microscopy, the conventional method of detecting malarial parasites, is very time consuming especially when tons of samples are needed to be analysed.

The application of image processing on the detection of malarial parasites greatly improves its efficiency by digitalisation and generating automatic analysis.
